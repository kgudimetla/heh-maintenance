Owner's Guide to the Hybrid Model

Why move away from "Pure SFT"?

Most housing societies adopt "Pure SFT" for simplicity, but it creates a fundamental imbalance in shared services. The Hybrid model corrects this by separating usage from ownership.

Principle 1: Service Equality

Security guards protect doors, not square footage. Every flat receives exactly one MyGate account and equal lift access. Therefore, these costs are shared equally (Fixed).

Principle 2: Asset Responsibility

Insurance, building exterior maintenance, and Diesel for backup power are tied to the size of the unit and its associated load. Owners of larger units pay a larger share here (UDS).

Fairness Checklist

  • Non-Discriminatory: Larger flats are not penalized for services that are independent of area.
  • Sustainable: Ensures smaller units contribute their fair share for the common infrastructure they use equally.
  • Legal Precedent: Many RWA laws (like KAOA) allow for specific expenses to be split by unit if defined as "Service Charges."

Final Impact: 3 BHK owners (median) see almost no change. 2.5 BHK owners contribute slightly more to the service base, while 4 BHK owners pay a more accurate reflection of their asset value without overpaying for services.
